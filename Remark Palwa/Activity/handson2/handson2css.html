The "Big Four" of grunge bands, consisting of Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains, collectively defined the grunge movement of the 1990s. Nirvana, fronted by the enigmatic Kurt Cobain, ignited a cultural revolution with their raw and angsty sound, epitomized by their landmark album "Nevermind." Pearl Jam, led by Eddie Vedder, embraced a more melodic and socially conscious approach, earning legions of devoted fans. Soundgarden, with Chris Cornell's powerful vocals, blended heavy metal and alternative rock, creating a unique sonic landscape. Meanwhile, Alice in Chains delved into darkness and addiction with their haunting melodies, personified by Layne Staley. Together, these four bands shaped a generation and left an indelible mark on the music world.
